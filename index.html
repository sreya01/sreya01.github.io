Parkinson's Disease Detection using Machine Learning
February 2022 - August 2022

Parkinson's disease is a chronic and progressive neurological disorder that affects movement and other motor functions. Early diagnosis is crucial for the effective management of the disease. Machine learning has shown potential for early diagnosis of Parkinson's disease by analyzing voice and speech patterns, tremors, and gait patterns. Various machine learning algorithms, such as support vector machines, decision trees, and neural networks, have been used for the detection of Parkinson's disease with high accuracy. Parkinson's disease detection using machine learning has the potential to improve the accuracy and speed of diagnosis, leading to better management of the disease and improved quality of life for patients.

Facial Recognition Systems using Dimensionality Reduction Algorithms
January 2022 - May 2022

Facial recognition systems have become increasingly popular in recent years due to their potential applications in a wide range of fields, including security, entertainment, and marketing. One of the major challenges in building such systems is to reduce the dimensionality of the feature space to accurately and efficiently identify and match faces. After reducing the dimensionality of the feature space, classification algorithms such as Support Vector Machines (SVM), Neural Networks, and k-Nearest Neighbors (k-NN) are used to classify and match the faces. The classification process involves comparing the feature vectors of the input image with the pre-stored feature vectors of the faces in the database.
